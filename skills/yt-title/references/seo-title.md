# YouTube title best practices

## Hard limits
- Maximum **100 characters**. Titles longer are rejected/truncated.

## Display / SEO
- Sweet spot **65–80 characters** — fits most surfaces (search, home, sidebar,
  mobile) without the "…" cutoff.
- **First ~40 characters matter most**: that's what survives truncation and where
  YouTube weights keywords heaviest. Put the primary keyword there.
- Mobile shows fewer characters than desktop; the front-load rule covers both.

## Writing
- One clear idea. A viewer should know what they'll get.
- Honest specificity beats vague hype — clickbait that the video doesn't deliver
  tanks watch time and hurts ranking.
- High-CTR levers: a concrete number ("7 ways…"), a clear benefit/outcome, a
  curiosity gap, a surprising claim, or a direct question.
- Avoid: ALL CAPS, excessive punctuation (!!!), keyword stuffing, brackets overload.

## The 5-type spectrum (sensational → refined)

Generate one of each so the creator sees the full risk/tone range:

1. **Bold / high-arousal** — strongest curiosity gap, emotion, or surprise.
   Highest CTR ceiling; risk: can read as clickbait or low-brow. Levers: a
   shocking-but-true number, "Nobody tells you…", a bold claim, a provocative
   question. Must still be honest.
2. **Punchy / mainstream** — energetic, broadly appealing, not cheap. The safe
   high-performer. Levers: concrete number + outcome, light curiosity.
3. **Clear / benefit-led** — plainly states what the viewer gains. Neutral,
   trustworthy tone.
4. **Refined / understated** — tasteful, credible, no hype. For expert,
   educational, premium, or sensitive topics.
5. **SEO / keyword-led** — exact search phrase front-loaded for discovery.

### Choosing the recommendation
- Match the content and audience: bold/punchy for browse & suggested traffic and
  entertainment; clear/SEO for search-driven evergreen; refined for expert/
  premium brands.
- **Sensitive topics (health, finance, news, legal): default to clear/refined**
  and explicitly flag the risk of the bold option.
- Every title must be deliverable by the video — sensational is fine, dishonest
  is not. Clickbait that underdelivers tanks watch time and ranking.
