# Trusted source domains

Only rely on sources from these categories for verification and references.
Prefer the **primary/original** source over any aggregator. This list is a
starting allowlist — extend it for the content's domain, but keep the bar:
authoritative, accountable, and citable.

## Government & intergovernmental
- `*.gov`, `*.gov.<country>` (e.g. gov.uk), `*.mil`
- `*.int`, `who.int`, `un.org`, `worldbank.org`, `imf.org`, `oecd.org`
- `europa.eu`, `ecdc.europa.eu`, `nasa.gov`, `noaa.gov`, `cdc.gov`, `nih.gov`,
  `fda.gov`, `census.gov`, `bls.gov`, `federalreserve.gov`

## Academic & scientific
- `*.edu`, `*.ac.<country>` (e.g. ac.uk)
- Peer-reviewed / publishers: `nature.com`, `science.org`, `cell.com`,
  `thelancet.com`, `nejm.org`, `pnas.org`, `ieee.org`, `acm.org`, `springer.com`,
  `sciencedirect.com`, `wiley.com`
- Indexes / repositories: `ncbi.nlm.nih.gov` (PubMed/PMC), `pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov`,
  `arxiv.org` (preprint — note "not peer-reviewed"), `doi.org`, `scholar.google.com`

## Major reference works
- `britannica.com`, `merriam-webster.com`
- `wikipedia.org` — acceptable as a **pointer**; verify against and cite its
  primary references rather than the article itself for contested facts.

## Established news & wires
- Wires: `reuters.com`, `apnews.com`, `afp.com`, `bloomberg.com`
- Outlets: `bbc.com`/`bbc.co.uk`, `npr.org`, `pbs.org`, `economist.com`,
  `nytimes.com`, `wsj.com`, `washingtonpost.com`, `theguardian.com`, `ft.com`,
  `nationalgeographic.com`

## Recognized fact-checkers
- `politifact.com`, `snopes.com`, `factcheck.org`, `fullfact.org`,
  `apnews.com/hub/ap-fact-check`, `reuters.com/fact-check`

## Domain-specific authorities (add as relevant)
- Finance: `sec.gov`, `federalreserve.gov`, company investor-relations filings
- Health: `who.int`, `cdc.gov`, `nih.gov`, `mayoclinic.org`, `cochranelibrary.com`
- Law: official court sites, `supremecourt.gov`, primary statute databases
- Tech specs/standards: `w3.org`, `ietf.org`, `iso.org`, official project docs

## Avoid for verification
- Anonymous blogs, content farms, SEO listicles, AI-generated aggregators
- Social media posts (use only to locate a primary source, never as the source)
- Sites with no named authorship/editorial accountability
- The script's own claims as evidence for themselves
